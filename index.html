Care Near You in California

Mental health disorders are among the most common health conditions faced by Californians: Nearly 1 in 6 California adults experience a mental illness of some kind, and 1 in 24 have a serious mental illness that makes it difficult to carry out major life activities.

California has about 2,100 mental health facilities throughout the state. Half of them are concentrated in Los Angeles, Orange and San Diego counties.

12% of counties in California do not have any mental health facilities, according to our analysis. Most of these counties are rural and sparsely populated.

Mendocino, Amador and Calaveras counties register the highest number of suicides per capita. All are rural areas with low population density.
