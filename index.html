Care Near You in California

By Kavish Harjai and Irene Casado Sánchez

California has over 2,100 mental health facilities throughout the state. More than half of them are concentrated in three urban areas: Los Angeles, Orange and San Diego. Meanwhile, 12% of counties –Alpine, Del Norte, Lassen, Madera, Modoc, Mono and Plumas– mainly rural and sparsely populated, do not have any mental health care facility, according to our analysis.

Mental health disorders are among the most common health conditions faced by Californians: Nearly 1 in 6 California adults experience a mental illness of some kind, and 1 in 24 have a serious mental illness that makes it difficult to carry out major life activities.

Solving mental health-related illnesses requires proper attention. But, about two-thirds of adults with a mental illness and two-thirds of adolescents with major depressive episodes did not get treatment in California in 2018.

Half of all Californians consider that their community does not have enough mental health providers to meet its needs, as a 2019 statewide poll of California residents shows.
